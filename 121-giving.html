121Giving

121Giving is the premier brand marketplace for good – driven by everyday giving, gifting, and community centered needs.

The founder was raising a second round of funding and wanted a modern, mobile-first campaign creation experience to sell to investors.

Contributions

UX Design, Interaction Design, Strategy, Visual Design

Team

Founder, Me

Timeline

1 month

Research

I began with a competitive analysis to pull out any features and functionality that may be expected for this platform.

I learned that most platforms focused primarily on facilitating the money gathering aspect of fund-raising. Few platforms provided guidance and assistance to create a meaningful story for influencing potential donors. The tech was a blank slate and not a helpful partner.

This insight guided the addition of pro-tips in the design. I also focused the design to promote video content for its emotional resonance and I included some options for communicating the severity of the need.

Process

I mapped out the user flow, created wireframes for it, and tested and iterated until the client and users were satisfied.

The initial wireframes and flow leaned too heavily into the idea of a marketplace for needs. While users didn't mind the marketplace idea the founder was displeased and felt I had confused his intentions. Subsequent iterations were more streamlined and less commerce-y in nature.

Results

The platform has an inviting layout to encourage user engagement. Clear calls-to-action and intiutive navigation reduces friction. Multiple sign-in options and a guest mode balance security with convenience. The green tones are calming and the clean design fosters trust and accessibility, creating a welcoming experience that promotes user retention.

These screens focus on defining campaign needs with a clear and actionable layout. The pro tips guide users, enhancing understanding and engagement. Cash goals provide transparency and control over their campaign. The wishlist showcases added items with pricing and discounts, helping givers see exactly how contributions will be utilized.

These screens present a dashboard for monitoring campaign progress. Displaying key metrics like percentage completion, givers, followers, and time remaining helps users track their campaign at a glance. Social sharing options and a campaign link amplify reach, encouraging engagement.

Here you're seeing the campaign creation steps and the focus on video storytelling. The options for adding media are flexible but it's known that video converts so the emphasis is placed there first.

Before launching a campaign the creator is shown what their page will look like. This receipt view creates transparency and minimzes errors and updates after the fact.

Items are perusable and can be added with a simple and streamlined in context interaction. When the campaign is launched the story of the donations is displayed adding an element of social proof and accountability to the campaign which builds trust with donors.
