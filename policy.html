AI-Augmented Care Operations Apprenticeship Program

A legislative and implementing policy framework for a new non-clinical healthcare workforce classification — designed to expand access, reduce clinician burnout, and ensure AI tools augment rather than replace human judgment.

Submitted for Policy Associate, Workforce of the Future
Organization Bay Area Council
Document type Legislative & Implementing Policy Framework
Role proposed ACO Apprentice — Non-Clinical Healthcare Worker
Behind the work

Why I wrote this policy

I currently coordinate medical referrals at HealthRIGHT 360, a primary care clinic serving people living with substance use disorders, mental health challenges, and housing instability. Every day I do work that is clearly valuable, clearly non-clinical, and clearly enabled by AI — insurance authorization, referral tracking, care coordination across systems.

There's no defined workforce classification for what I do. No credential pathway. No policy framework that acknowledges this labor category exists. That's a problem — not just for workers like me, but for the millions of patients whose care depends on this work being done well.

This policy is my attempt to define the lane: what ACO Apprentices can do, what they can't, how AI tools get governed, and how we prevent this from becoming a new exploitable tier of healthcare labor. The framework is explicitly designed to protect clinical scope of practice while creating real advancement pathways for workers entering through apprenticeship.

I built this because I've lived both sides of the workforce development problem — I found my way back into employment through an OEWD apprenticeship after being displaced by the same AI wave this policy is designed to govern. That's the perspective I'd bring to this committee.

Policy Framework

8 sections · ACO Apprentice Classification
I
Purpose & Legislative Intent
Establishes a regulated non-clinical workforce classification to expand patient access and reduce licensed staff burnout — without substituting technology for clinical judgment.
II
Authorized Classification
Role: AI-Augmented Care Operations Apprentice (ACO Apprentice)
  • Non-clinical healthcare employee
  • Registered or employer-sponsored apprenticeship
  • W-2 employee status required — no independent contractor classification
III
Scope of Work
Permitted:
  • Insurance eligibility & authorization follow-up
  • Billing & coding prep (pre-submission only)
  • Documentation drafting — factual transcription only
  • Scheduling coordination & care logistics
  • Navigation between EHR, payer portals, and scheduling systems
IV
Supervision, Accountability & Liability
All AI-assisted outputs tied to patient records must be reviewed and approved by a licensed professional before final use. Legal and financial liability rests with the employing healthcare organization — not the apprentice.
  • Mandatory human oversight on all AI-assisted outputs
  • AI tools treated as internal productivity aids, not clinical decision-support systems
V
AI Tool Governance & Data Protection
Only HIPAA-compliant, auditable, state-approved AI tools permitted. All AI interactions must be logged, time-stamped, and user-attributed.
  • Patient data may not be used for AI model training
  • Vendors prohibited from retaining or exporting identifiable data
  • Cross-border data processing prohibited unless explicitly authorized
VI
Labor Standards & Worker Protections
  • Wages must meet or exceed local living-wage benchmarks
  • Completion maps to recognized credentials or advancement pathways
  • Role may not be used to replace licensed staff or suppress wages
  • Employers must track demographic participation and advancement
VII
Pilot Structure & Evaluation
Initial pilots limited to 90–180 days per site. Authorization expires unless explicitly renewed based on demonstrated improvements.
  • Patient access and wait times
  • Licensed staff time allocation
  • Documentation backlog and claim denial rates
  • Apprentice retention and advancement rates
VIII
Legal Interpretation Rule
This policy shall be interpreted narrowly. Nothing may be construed to expand clinical scope of practice, delegate clinical judgment, weaken licensure requirements, or authorize unsupervised use of AI in patient care.

Any ambiguity in task scope defaults to prohibition, not authorization.
⚠

Automatic Stop-Loss Triggers

Program is immediately suspended if any of the following occur:

Patient safety events increase beyond baseline Compliance or privacy breach Task scope expands beyond authorization Workforce displacement identified
