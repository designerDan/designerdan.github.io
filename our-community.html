Our Community

Our Community is a mobile app for creating communities of good by engaging in activities with members of your various social circles.

This project was a personal wish of one Midwest banking magnate to turn their home community into the American midwest's first blue zone.

Contributions

UX Design, Interaction Design, Strategy, Visual Design

Team

Product Owner, Engineering Consultant, Me

Timeline

3 months

Research

Research was primarily through informal interviews and testing cycles i.e. show some designs and get some feedback on them.

You might think that when looking for something to do the most important factor is what you'll be doing and you'd be right...when you're looking for entertainment.

When you're looking to build a community then it is the who that solves the equation. Who do you want to get together with? Who will be there? After all, a concert with strangers is different than a concert with friends or family and is a large portion of the process of deciding whether to go or not.

Process

Getting to know the audience was the prime directive. From there I used scenarios and create flows through the app appropriate to the scenario's context.

The benefit of this approach is that it is human-centric, it leverages ambiguity, and it considers multiple journeys of the same person across multiple levels of their life.

This particular project began with a proof of concept sprint that was greenlit into a larger initiative. Here you're seeing the beginning concept and a few iterations of the larger effort. You'll note that the increase in fidelity as the best ideas and interactions from early prototypes were refined and polished.

Results

One common concern we heard about our competitors is that they often have too many events to sift through and nearly none are relevant. To address this concern in this product we provided filters and a tagging system making it is easy to find and surface meaningful activities.

Because many of our creator audience are business or organization leaders and because businesses and organizations are often cornerstones of any community we provide them with representation in the platform through secondary profiles. By treating businesses and organizations as sub-profiles of individuals we make space for them but keep the focus on everyday people.

Places are an important twin to activities as no activity will ever take place (see?) anywhere but some manner of location. Our version of places provides for community interaction through tagging thereby allowing our creators insight into where best to host their activities.

Providing the ability to search through the app helps users navigate and easily find what they want. Because this app constrains the information it can contain this search is streamlined and highly intuitive.

Lists help people organize and customize their app experience beyond what the technology is capable of. Notifications keep users up to date on the comings and goings, quickly linking to important actions and connecting users to their social circles.

Personal touches like video invites increase the odds someone will use the app but more importantly to the purpose, show up and engage with the community.
