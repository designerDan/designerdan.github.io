New-Voters

Overview

The organization had an existing PDF-driven process which they wanted to consolidate into an interactive and engaging website.

Timeline

3+ months

Team

Product Owner, Engineer, Me

Contributions

UX Design, Strategy

Research & Insights

My research for this project consisted of stakeholder interviews, literature reviews, and YouTube video watching.

I set out to learn the needs and behaviors of the audience. What I came away with was a list of functional and qualitative requirements for the design.

The driving insight was that their audience feels overwhelmed and poorly supported in their efforts so a software the keeps actions bite-sized, simple, and as easy as possible would be beneficial.

Process

For a quick dive into my process, you'll see I architected the prototype as a journey across time, contexts, and players.

The benefit of this approach is making known the interactions between the variables. Designing for multiple users across multiple instances requires choreographing the dance. You're seeing a small part of what that dance looks like.

I captured the emergent architecture in models and components, alighting each model with spacing and alignment grids to communicate structure and allow for work at the system level.

Results

You'll note the experience is one of progress, achievement, and ease. Big numbers going up. Knocking down walls. Getting things done. Nothing takes longer than it needs to to complete. Small pieces over time, you can power through, we provide for flow if you want.

Due to chunking, progress happens at different levels, The larger, smaller, and in-between. Accommodating for that demands progress bars. A big benefit of this approach is you're always aware of your place within the whole.

Talk about flow, to help you quickly make progress across multiple tasks I designed, what I call the interior, pages to be about flow. You're always going next, when you get to the end of a task, instead of backing out and jumping back in you can proceed immediately into the next task. This coming in and going out of flow helps any long stretches of work feel less long.

Here you're getting any overview of the main interior interactions, the lowest level, the questions on the forms, expressed here on the website with supporting interactions. The goal is to make this experience less a data entry and checkbox experience and more a click and flow experience.

Some extra benefit on display here are a focus on communicating the status of the system with an estimated time to complete and a completed signifier at the end, which, should the user ever return to this segment they will know it is complete and they can find their way to the next thing to be done.

What you're seeing in the interface here is a compact snapshot of the Mentor experience. The experience once again being a contextual one.

Delivering What you need to know, when you need to know it is how you limit cognitive load and make these brain-based experiences less effortful.

Impact

Of the 5 users I tested with 5 of them were more satisfied with this experience than the current one.

Everything about this is great. We're excited to use it.

– User Alum
